Plant Disease Library

Find detailed information on various plant diseases, their symptoms, treatments, and prevention methods.

Powdery Mildew

A fungal disease that affects many plants, causing a white powdery coating on leaves and stems.

Treatment:
  • Apply fungicide like sulfur or neem oil.
  • Remove infected leaves.
Prevention:
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants for air circulation.
  • Water at the base of plants to avoid wetting the leaves.

Leaf Spot

A bacterial or fungal infection that causes spots on the leaves, leading to yellowing and early leaf drop.

Treatment:
  • Use copper-based fungicides or bactericides.
  • Prune affected leaves and dispose of them properly.
Prevention:
  • Water in the morning to allow leaves to dry before nightfall.
  • Ensure proper spacing for air circulation.

Blight

A disease caused by various fungi or bacteria that affects leaves, stems, and fruits, causing rapid tissue decay.

Treatment:
  • Apply fungicides or bactericides specific to the cause of the blight.
  • Remove infected plants and debris.
Prevention:
  • Practice crop rotation to avoid soil-borne pathogens.
  • Water plants at the base to avoid wetting the foliage.

Root Rot

A disease caused by fungi or bacteria that attack the roots, causing the plant to wilt and eventually die.

Treatment:
  • Remove infected plants and dispose of them properly.
  • Use a fungicide for root rot treatment.
Prevention:
  • Ensure proper drainage in the soil.
  • Avoid over-watering plants.

Rust

A fungal disease characterized by reddish-brown spots on the leaves, causing the plant to weaken.

Treatment:
  • Apply fungicides such as copper-based or systemic fungicides.
  • Remove and destroy infected leaves.
Prevention:
  • Practice crop rotation to avoid rust build-up.
  • Ensure good air circulation around plants.

Anthracnose

A fungal disease that affects fruits, flowers, and stems, causing dark lesions and dieback.

Treatment:
  • Use fungicides to control the spread.
  • Remove infected plant parts and destroy them.
Prevention:
  • Water plants early in the day.
  • Provide good airflow to reduce moisture levels.

Apple Scab

A fungal disease that affects apples, causing dark, scabby lesions on fruit and leaves.

Treatment:
  • Apply fungicides like captan or mancozeb.
  • Prune infected branches and dispose of fallen leaves.
Prevention:
  • Plant resistant apple varieties.
  • Ensure good air circulation through proper pruning.

Coffee Rust

A fungal disease that causes yellow spots on leaves, leading to defoliation and reduced coffee yield.

Treatment:
  • Apply systemic fungicides such as triazoles.
  • Remove and destroy infected leaves.
Prevention:
  • Plant rust-resistant coffee varieties.
  • Maintain optimal soil fertility for stronger plants.

Tomato Early Blight

A fungal disease that causes dark spots on leaves, stems, and fruit, weakening the plant.

Treatment:
  • Apply fungicides such as chlorothalonil or copper-based sprays.
  • Remove infected plant material.
Prevention:
  • Rotate crops to reduce fungal spores in the soil.
  • Water at the base of plants to avoid leaf wetting.

Peach Bacterial Spot

A bacterial disease that causes dark spots on leaves and fruit, reducing peach quality.

Treatment:
  • Use copper-based bactericides during early stages.
  • Remove and dispose of infected fruit and leaves.
Prevention:
  • Plant resistant peach varieties.
  • Provide adequate airflow around trees by proper pruning.

Corn Common Rust

A fungal disease that produces rust-colored pustules on corn leaves, reducing yield.

Treatment:
  • Apply fungicides like propiconazole or pyraclostrobin.
  • Remove infected leaves if possible.
Prevention:
  • Plant resistant corn varieties.
  • Practice crop rotation to avoid fungal build-up in soil.

Grape Black Rot

A fungal disease that causes black spots on grapes, leaves, and stems, leading to crop loss.

Treatment:
  • Apply fungicides such as mancozeb or myclobutanil.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant material.
Prevention:
  • Prune vines to improve airflow and sunlight exposure.
  • Use fungicide sprays during wet seasons.
